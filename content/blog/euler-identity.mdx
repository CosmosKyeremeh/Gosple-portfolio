Euler's Identity is declared the most beautiful equation in mathematics:

$$e^{i\pi} + 1 = 0$$

Five constants. One equation. But beauty without understanding is decoration.

## The Exponential as a Rotation Machine

The Taylor expansion of $e^{i\theta}$ separates cleanly into real and imaginary parts:

$$e^{i\theta} = \underbrace{\left(1 - \frac{\theta^2}{2!} + \frac{\theta^4}{4!} - \cdots\right)}_{\cos\theta} + i\underbrace{\left(\theta - \frac{\theta^3}{3!} + \frac{\theta^5}{5!} - \cdots\right)}_{\sin\theta}$$

This is **Euler's Formula**: $e^{i\theta} = \cos\theta + i\sin\theta$.

## Why $\theta = \pi$ Gives $-1$

Setting $\theta = \pi$:

$$e^{i\pi} = \cos\pi + i\sin\pi = -1 + 0i$$

Geometrically: start at $(1, 0)$ on the unit circle, rotate by $\pi$ radians, land at $(-1, 0)$.

$$e^{i\pi} + 1 = 0$$

The identity is **not mysterious**. It is the algebraic statement of a half-turn.

## Why This Matters Computationally

The Discrete Fourier Transform uses complex exponentials as its basis functions:

$$X[k] = \sum_{n=0}^{N-1} x[n] \cdot e^{-i \cdot 2\pi k n / N}$$

Every frequency component is a rotating phasor. Euler's formula is why signal decomposition works.
